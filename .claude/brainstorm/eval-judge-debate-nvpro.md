The decision matrix for the v0.6.0 evaluation judge requires prioritizing scientific rigor over infrastructure experimentation. While the temptation to utilize the free, high-capacity `nv_pro` backend is strong—especially given the project's trajectory toward local-first autonomy—it fundamentally undermines the specific objective of this release phase: measuring delta improvement against the v0.5.1 baseline.

The core tension lies in baseline comparability. The v0.5.1 failure (3/3 tiers below 4.0) was established using Anthropic's Sonnet-class models. To claim v0.6.0 succeeds where v0.5.1 failed, the measurement instrument must remain constant. Switching to `nv_pro` (Option B) or `gem` (Option C) introduces a confounding variable. If scores rise, we cannot discern whether the improvement stems from the new thinking-mode defaults or merely from a different judge's scoring distribution. As noted in the brief, validating a new judge would require re-running v0.5.1 fixtures, effectively doubling the evaluation workload and delaying the release without adding commensurate value to the v0.6.0 specific hypothesis.

Regarding the "self-family" bias: yes, it is material. Using a Qwen3.5-397b model to judge outputs generated by Qwen3 variants introduces a systemic risk of over-correlation. The judge may implicitly favor tokenization patterns, reasoning styles, or structural idiosyncrasies common to the Qwen family that an out-of-family judge (like Sonnet or Gemini) would penalize or ignore. For a release claiming objective performance gains, this internal bias is unacceptable.

Furthermore, the project history indicates a PM who rejects "lazy" shortcuts but values discipline. While Option B is technically "free," choosing it here to avoid a $1–3 cost constitutes a different kind of laziness: avoiding the intellectual debt of maintaining experimental continuity. The cost of the Anthropic API is negligible compared to the engineering hours spent debating the validity of the results. Switching judges should be reserved for v0.7+, driven by a principled architectural shift toward fully local evaluation pipelines, rather than as an ad-hoc cost-saving measure during a critical regression check.

Therefore, we must maintain the status quo to ensure the integrity of the comparative data. The script is ready, the baseline is defined, and the cost is trivial.

**Recommendation: Option A**
